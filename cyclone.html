Cyclone Preparedness

Learn about hurricane and cyclone preparedness, tracking systems, and comprehensive safety measures.

Before a Cyclone

Monitor weather forecasts and official cyclone tracking updates

Prepare emergency supplies: water, food, medications, flashlights

Secure your home: board windows, clear gutters, trim trees

Know your evacuation zone and routes

During a Cyclone

Follow evacuation orders immediately - don't wait

Stay in the strongest part of your building, away from windows

Beware of the eye - winds will return from opposite direction

Stay indoors until authorities declare it safe to go outside

Cyclone Categories

Cat 1:

74-95 mph winds - Minimal damage, some power outages

Cat 2:

96-110 mph winds - Moderate damage, extensive power outages

Cat 3:

111-129 mph winds - Extensive damage, major power outages

Cat 4:

130-156 mph winds - Extreme damage, long-term power outages

Cat 5:

157+ mph winds - Catastrophic damage, complete power failure

After a Cyclone

Wait for official all-clear before leaving shelter

Avoid flooded roads and downed power lines

Document damage with photos for insurance claims

Be patient - recovery and restoration take time
